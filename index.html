About

Victoria Sharpe was born and raised in Richmond, Virginia. As a kid, all she ever wanted to be was an “Artist”. In an attempt to narrow down her focus, she attended Virginia Commonwealth University and earned a Bachelor of Fine Arts in Photography and Film with a minor in General Business.


While in college, Sharpe taught science shows, camps, and workshops to elementary and middle-school aged students and fell in love with education. For the past five years, she has taken her passion for teaching, science, and the media arts and taught Photography and Film classes at Grace and Hope Academy in Petersburg, Virginia, tutored English and Math in Henrico and Richmond Public Schools, and currently teaches VCU's Department of Photography + Film's Summer Film Camp. In and out of the school year, she is also a freelance video editor, videographer, and photographer.


As a creative and entrepreneur, having a portfolio to show your work is a necessity. Because web development is so versatile and a part of every industry, Sharpe is currently pursuing a certificate at University of Richmond for Full Stack Web Development.


In her spare time, Sharpe enjoys watching movies, replaying The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild, traveling, camping, and playing D&D.

Hold My Beer

Hold My Beer is a mobile responsive web app that allows users to search a state or city/state for breweries fitting their choice of size (brewpubs, microbreweries, regional, and domestic breweries) and if the establishment is currently open or not.

Cabbage Collector

Cabbage Collector is a web browser game where the player tries to collect yummy cabbages! Each type of cabbage is worth a different amount of points. The goal is to collect the same number of cabbages as the target number without going over. Good luck!

GitHub

Train Time

Train Time is a Google Firebase web app that allows users to add trains to a list of currently "running" trains. By recieving the first train arrival time and the train's frequency, the app will calculate using Moment.js the train's next arrival time and the number of minutes until it arrives.

GitHub

LIRI

LIRI is a command line node app that works similar to iOS's SIRI. LIRI takes in parameters given by the user and gives them back data. The user can choose from four different commands to pull up information about a song, a movie, an artist/band's concert dates, and sample searches from a .txt file.
